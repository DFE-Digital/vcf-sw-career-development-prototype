Leaving care

Summary
Ensure the safety of care leavers and provide the support they need to lead independent and fulfilling lives

What you'll do

Your responsibilities will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in leaving care teams are expected to:

  • support young people leaving care and develop plans that address their needs and goals
  • help the wider networks of care leavers provide them with stability and consistency
  • help care leavers access financial support, accommodation, education and employment
  • help care leavers develop essential life skills such as cooking and budgeting
  • use approaches such as life story work to help care leavers develop their identity and identify aspirations
  • help care leavers stay connected with supportive adults such as family members and former key workers
  • support care leavers with looking after their health and well-being
  • provide ongoing advice and support as care leavers settle into independent living

Skills and knowledge

The skills and knowledge you need will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in leaving care teams are expected to have:

  • expertise in child safeguarding and assessing and managing risk
  • skills in carrying out assessments and developing personalised support plans
  • an understanding of relevant legislation and policies relating to leaving care
  • the ability to teach life skills such as budgeting and cooking
  • the ability to support care leavers to maintain positive relationships with their wider network
  • an understanding of the process of transitioning from child to adult social care
  • knowledge of financial support and education grants available to care leavers
  • interpersonal skills to engage with young people clearly and compassionately
  • the ability to work effectively with young people from diverse backgrounds and communities

Who you'll work with

You could work with external partners and services including:

  • health services
  • schools
  • mental health services
  • residential care providers
  • employment specialists
  • housing departments

How you'll work

Working conditions can vary, but you could:

  • be office-based on work remotely, or a combination of both
  • work full-time or part-time
  • work flexible hours, compressed hours or have a job share
  • travel?
  • support systems in place for the team, such as supervision, training, and access to resources?
