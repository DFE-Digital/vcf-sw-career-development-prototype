Areas of practice

Adolescents

Summary
Support families and children facing challenges and risks during adolescence
Other names
Adolescent support team, integrated adolescent service, youth support service

What you'll do

Your responsibilities will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in adolescents teams are expected to:

  • work with children and families to assess their needs, challenges and circumstances
  • carry out direct work with children to understand their feelings and wishes
  • identify familial and extra-familial risks including exploitation and violence
  • carry out home visits to assess living conditions and family dynamics
  • develop and implement safety plans where children are at risk of harm
  • empower children and families to address conflicts and positively manage their relationships
  • identify mental health or substance abuse risks and refer to specialist teams
  • support children to develop themselves and access education and training
  • support children to develop essential life skills such as budgeting, cooking and self-care

Skills and knowledge

The skills and knowledge you need will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in adolescents teams are expected to have:

  • interpersonal skills to communicate with children clearly and compassionately
  • an understanding of the physical, cognitive, emotional, and social development of children
  • the ability to assess risks to children’s well-being and protect them from harm
  • the ability to present evidence in court concisely and clearly
  • an understanding of extra-familial risk and exploitation of children
  • skills in carrying out assessments and developing personalised support plans
  • knowledge of mental health and substance abuse issues affecting adolescents and appropriate interventions
  • an understanding of education and training opportunities to help children maximise their potential
  • the ability to work effectively with children from diverse backgrounds and communities

It could also be useful to have a qualification from a specialised training programme in:

  • mental health support
  • substance abuse support
  • therapeutic interventions

Who you'll work with

You could work with external partners and services including:

  • youth justice services
  • police
  • mental health services
  • substance abuse services
  • health services
  • schools
  • community support organisations
