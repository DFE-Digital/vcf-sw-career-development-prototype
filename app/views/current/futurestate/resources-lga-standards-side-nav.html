The Local Government Association Standards for Employers of Social Workers in England

The standards set out the shared core expectations of employers which will enable social workers in all employment settings to work effectively and safely.

Introduction

These Standards should be viewed as part of a “whole systems” approach to supporting practitioners in their practice. The Employer Standards should also be aligned with your own organisational policies and practices as mechanisms that support the wellbeing of practitioners and enable them to flourish and do good quality work. The Employer Standards (ES) are assessed through an annual ‘Health Check’ administered in Local Authorities and other social work-related employer organisations. The Health Check is a survey of social workers, approved mental health professionals, occupational therapists, and non-registered social care practitioners (henceforth referred to as practitioners). It is designed to provide important information on how the workforce perceives their practice conditions and aspects of their working environment. The findings provide insight into the challenges you face in the areas identified by the ES and identify areas for improvement.

Resources for leaders

These resources are designed to support you as leaders to respond to your organisation’s Health Check scores against each of the Standards. They aim to support you in implementing strategies that will enhance working conditions and optimise organisational culture. These actions will help to improve organisational functioning, workforce wellbeing, potentially enhance recruitment and retention and are likely to have a positive impact on direct practice. The resources also aim to inform organisational level improvements through subsequent waves of the Health Check and provide evidence of action planning for improvement.

Standards for Employers of Social Workers in England

  • 1. Strong and clear social work framework
  • 2. Effective workforce planning systems
  • 3. Safe workloads and case allocation
  • 4. Wellbeing
  • 5. Supervision
  • 6. Continuing professional development
  • 7. Professional registration
  • 8. Strategic partnerships
