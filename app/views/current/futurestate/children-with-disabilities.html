Areas of practice

Children with disabilities

Summary
Support the well-being, independence and rights of children with disabilities and their families
Other names
Special educational needs and disabilities (SEND)

What you'll do

Your responsibilities will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in children with disabilities teams are expected to:

  • carry out assessments and develop support plans for children with disabilities
  • review support plans to ensure they meet the needs of children
  • advocate for children and their families to ensure their voices are heard and their rights upheld
  • support families to access available resources, support networks and financial help
  • arrange for specialist equipment to be provided to children and families
  • support children to develop skills that promote independence and participation in their communities
  • work with multi-agency partners to ensure support is joined-up and consistent
  • involve children and families in developing their support packages
  • help children prepare for adulthood and independence

Skills and knowledge

The skills and knowledge you need will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in children with disabilities teams are expected to have:

  • knowledge of legislation relevant to children with disabilities
  • expertise in child safeguarding and assessing and managing potential risk
  • knowledge of child development and the impact of disabilities on physical and emotional development
  • familiarity with assessment frameworks and tools used to evaluate the needs and progress of children with disabilities
  • knowledge of a range of interventions and therapeutic approaches
  • the ability to work collaboratively with other professionals and external partners
  • communication skills to engage with children and families clearly and compassionately
  • knowledge of available support services, community resources and financial help
  • the ability to work effectively with children and families from diverse backgrounds and communities

Who you'll work with

You could work with external partners and services including:

  • health services
  • occupational therapists
  • schools
  • mental health services
  • residential care providers

How you'll work

Working conditions can vary, but you could:

  • be office-based on work remotely, or a combination of both
  • work full-time or part-time
  • work flexible hours, compressed hours or have a job share
  • travel?
  • support systems in place for the team, such as supervision, training, and access to resources?
