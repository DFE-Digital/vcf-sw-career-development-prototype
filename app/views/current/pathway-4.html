Senior managers and practice leaders

Pathway 4: practice leaders

For
Current and aspiring practice leaders, directors, assistant directors, principal social workers
Duration
12 months
Time commitment
About 11 days (including 2 residential components)
Cost

Free (except for travel for the residential components)

Provider website
Frontline

Who it’s for

Pathway 4 is for:

  • in-role practice leaders
  • experienced heads of service or equivalent who want to become a practice leader

What you’ll learn

Pathway 4 will help you develop as a leader and champion of services across your local system. It covers themes including:

  • leading innovation across service areas and advocating for system change
  • using the latest research and best practice to improve services
  • implementing anti-racist and anti-oppressive practices
  • using data to measure success and manage risk
  • allocating budget and resource across multiple services

What you’ll get

When you finish Pathway 4 you’ll get:

  • a certificate which can contribute towards your continuous professional development (CPD) hours
  • access to leadership networks, including becoming part of the Frontline Fellowship community of leaders

It'll help prepare:

  • current practice leaders to access the UPON programme for directors of children’s services
  • aspiring practice leaders for promotion to practice leader roles

How it’s delivered

Pathway 4 has a time commitment of about 11 days spread over 12 months and includes in-person and online learning.

In-person learning

Includes:

  • 2 residential components at the University of Warwick (4 days total)
  • shadowing days

Online learning

Includes:

  • workshops
  • one-to-one coaching
  • peer-led practice groups
  • self-directed study sessions

Online modules last 1.5 to 3 hours each and happen several times a month.

Funding

Pathway 4 is funded by the Department for Education.

However, travel costs for the on-site days are the responsibility of your employer.

Next steps

Speak to your line manager to get support for you doing Pathway 4.

If you’re an aspiring practice leader, you’ll also need to answer questions about your work experience as part of your application.

Find out more about Pathway 4 including how to apply on the Frontline website.
