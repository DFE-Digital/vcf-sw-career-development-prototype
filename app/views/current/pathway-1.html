Practice supervisors

Pathway 1: practice supervisors

For
Practice supervisors, senior or consultant social workers, assistant team managers, advanced practitioners, practice educators
Duration
3 months
Time commitment
30 hours (the equivalent of 4 days)
Cost

>Free except for travel for in-person modules

Delivered by
Frontline

Who it’s for

Pathway 1 is for senior social workers who supervise the day-to-day work of child and family practitioners.

What you’ll learn

Pathway 1 will help you develop excellent social work practice and leadership in others, with a focus on:

  • prioritising the mental health and wellbeing of practitioners
  • promoting a team culture of open and honest feedback
  • putting in place high-quality reflective supervision
  • managing competing demands

What you’ll get

When you finish Pathway 1 you’ll get:

  • a certificate which can contribute towards your continuous professional development (CPD) hours
  • ongoing access to the practice supervisor network to support your development

It also prepares you for accessing Pathway 2 for middle managers.

How it’s delivered

Pathway 1 is a 3-month programme that:

  • runs 3 times a year starting in November, February and May
  • takes about 30 hours to complete
  • combines online and in-person learning

In-person learning

There are 2 days on-site at the University of Warwick.

Online learning

It'll take about 14 hours to complete the online modules, which include:

  • workshops
  • one-to-one coaching
  • self-directed study sessions

Online modules last 1.5 to 3 hours.

Funding

Pathway 1 is fully funded by the Department for Education.

However, travel costs for the residential component are the responsibility of your employer.

Next steps

Speak to your line manager to get support for you doing Pathway 1.

Find out more about Pathway 1 including how to apply on the Frontline website.
