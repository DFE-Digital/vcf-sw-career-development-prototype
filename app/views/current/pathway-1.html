DfE development programmes

Pathway 1: practice supervisors

For
Practice supervisors, team managers, lead practitioners
Duration
3 months
Time commitment
About 4 days (including a residential component)
Cost

Free (except for travel for the residential component)

Website
Frontline (opens in new tab)

Who it’s for

This programme is for experienced social workers who supervise the day-to-day work of child and family practitioners.

What you’ll learn

Pathway 1 will help you:

  • develop your leadership skills
  • prioritise the mental health and wellbeing of the practitioners you supervise
  • develop a team culture of open and honest feedback and reflection
  • put in place high-quality reflective supervision
  • develop your prioritisation skills to manage competing demands

What you’ll get

On completion of Pathway 1 you’ll get:

  • a certificate which can contribute towards your continuous professional development (CPD) hours
  • ongoing access to the practice supervisor network to support your development

Completing this programme prepares you for accessing Pathway 2 for middle managers.

How it’s delivered

This 3-month programme has a time commitment of about 4 days.

It combines online and in-person learning, including 2 days and nights on-site at a conference centre.

You'll take part in:

  • one-to-one coaching sessions
  • online workshops
  • self-directed study sessions

Funding

Pathway 1 is fully funded by the Department for Education.

However, your employer must cover travel costs for the residential component.

Next steps

Find out more about Pathway 1 on the Frontline website (opens in a new window).
