Newly qualified social workers

Assessed and supported year in employment (ASYE)

The Assessed and Supported Year in Employment for child and family services (ASYE) is a year-long programme that helps newly qualified social workers (NQSWs) develop their skills, knowledge and professional confidence.

The ASYE is open to all NQSWs in local authorities, and in the private and voluntary sectors. It is not mandatory, but many employers require it.

Registration and requirements

The following people can take the ASYE:

  • NQSWs employed through an agency
  • Social workers, registered with Social Work England and in their first two years of work
  • Social workers who have been qualified for up to four years who can satisfy their employer that their knowledge and experience is up to date

To qualify, you will be assessed against the Knowledge and Skills for Social Workers for Child and Family social work (KSS) [PDF]. This will identify your learning needs and inform your personal development plan (PDP).

The ASYE programme

Regular supervision by your assessor will happen

  • weekly during weeks 1 to 6
  • every two weeks from 6 weeks to 6 months
  • every month for the last 6 months

You will have half a day per week of protected development time, and your workload will be 10% less than an experienced social worker’s. You will also be able to shadow experienced social workers and go on placements.

Evaluation and assessment

A qualified social worker will be assigned to assess your progress through your ASYE. They will use the Professional Capability Framework and the KSS to guide them.

Evidence of your professional development will be collected through

  • 3 direct observations
  • 3 pieces of feedback from children and young people who need care and support
  • 3 pieces of feedback from other professionals
  • assessment of 3 written reports

You will also be asked to complete critical reflections logs after 3, 6 and 12 months. Your employer can also request an optional 9-month review of your progress.

At the end of the year your assessor will write a final assessment which your employer will use to decide whether you have passed or failed.
