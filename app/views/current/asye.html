Overview

The assessed and supported year in employment (ASYE) programme helps newly qualified social workers develop their skills, knowledge and professional confidence.

ASYE is open to all newly qualified social workers employed in the public, private and voluntary sectors.

It's not mandatory for child and family social workers, but a lot of employers require it.

How you're assessed

You’ll be assessed by a qualified social worker against the:

  • post-qualifying standards for child and family practitioners
  • professional capabilities framework for newly qualified social workers

Evidence of your professional development will be collected through:

  • direct observations
  • feedback from children and young people who need care and support
  • feedback from other professionals
  • assessment of written reports

You’ll also need to complete critical reflection logs after 3, 6 and 12 months. Your employer can also request an optional 9-month review of your progress.

At the end of the year your assessor will write a final assessment which your employer will use to decide whether you’ve passed or failed.

How you're supported

During your ASYE you'll:

  • receive regular supervision
  • work a reduced caseload
  • time for critical reflection

Your assessor will supervise you:

  • weekly during weeks 1 to 6
  • fortnightly from 6 weeks to 6 months
  • monthly for the last 6 months

You'll have half a day per week of protected development time, and your workload will be 10% less than an experienced social worker’s.

You'll also be able to shadow experienced social workers and go on placements.
