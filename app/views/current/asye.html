Overview

The assessed and supported year in employment (ASYE) programme helps newly qualified social workers develop their skills, knowledge and professional confidence.

It's for all newly qualified social workers employed in the public, private and voluntary sector.

It's not mandatory for child and family social workers, but a lot of employers require you to do it.

What you'll learn

ASYE will offer you the opportunity to:

  • receive regular supervision
  • work a reduced caseload
  • develop your capabilities and confidence

It will help you work towards your professional goals.

It also provides you with the opportunity to consolidate your learning from your bachelors or master’s degree in social work.

What you’ll get

By the end of the programme, you will have an ASYE portfolio which can evidence your learning.

How it’s delivered

Regular supervision by your assessor will happen:

  • weekly during weeks 1 to 6
  • every two weeks from 6 weeks to 6 months
  • every month for the last 6 months

You'll have half a day per week of protected development time, and your workload will be 10% less than an experienced social worker’s.

You'll also be able to shadow experienced social workers and go on placements.

How you're assessed

A qualified social worker will be assigned to assess your progress through your ASYE. They will use the Professional Capability Framework and the KSS to guide them.

Evidence of your professional development will be collected through:

  • 3 direct observations
  • 3 pieces of feedback from children and young people who need care and support
  • 3 pieces of feedback from other professionals
  • assessment of 3 written reports

You’ll also need to complete critical reflection logs after 3, 6 and 12 months. Your employer can also request an optional 9-month review of your progress.

At the end of the year your assessor will write a final assessment which your employer will use to decide whether you’ve passed or failed.
