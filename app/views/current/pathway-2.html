Middle managers and heads of services

Pathway 2: middle managers

For
Current and aspiring middle managers, team managers, independent reviewing officers (IROs)
Duration
10 months
Time commitment
About 7 days (including 2 residential components)
Cost

Free (except for travel for the residential components)

Website
Frontline (opens in new tab)

Who it’s for

Pathway 2 is for:

  • qualified social workers who manage practice supervisors but are not heads of service
  • experienced practice supervisors who want to become team managers

What you’ll learn

Pathway 2 will help you support the delivery of excellent social work practice within and beyond your team. This includes:

  • applying your leadership skills to increase the impact of your team
  • developing resilience and wellbeing in staff so they can do their best work
  • managing complex scenarios with your team and multi-agency partners
  • building an inclusive team culture

What you’ll get

When you finish Pathway 2 you’ll get:

  • a certificate which can contribute towards your continuous professional development (CPD) hours
  • access to leadership networks, including becoming part of the Frontline Fellowship community of leaders

Completing this programme helps prepare:

  • current middle managers to access Pathway 3 for heads of service
  • aspiring middle managers for promotion to middle manager roles

How it’s delivered

This 10-month programme has a time commitment of about 7 days.

It combines online and in-person learning, including 2 residential components days and nights on-site at a conference centre.

You'll take part in:

  • one-to-one coaching sessions
  • online workshops
  • self-directed study sessions

Funding

Pathway 2 is fully funded by the Department for Education.

However, travel costs for the residential components are the responsibility of your employer.

How to apply

If you’re currently a middle manager, you’ll need your line manager’s support to do this programme.

If you’re a practice supervisor who wants to become a middle manager, you’ll need to:

  • get your line manager’s support
  • answer questions about your work experience to support your application

Apply for Pathway 2 for middle managers on the Frontline website (opens in a new window).
