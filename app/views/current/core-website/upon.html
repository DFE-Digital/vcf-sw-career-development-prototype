DfE development programmes

Upon: inspiring leaders programme

The Aspirant Directors programme (ADP) for children’s services is aimed at senior managers who wish to be become directors within the next 3 years.

It is aimed at managers who wish to develop their leadership and management skills, technical knowledge, and confidence.

Registration and requirements

There are 40 places available on a single course which runs from autumn to spring.

We will give priority to those who have the potential to become a director in the next 3 years, but if you are interested, apply anyway because we are building up a talent pool for the future.

As part of the application process, you will be assessed on:

  • Your knowledge of children's services
  • How you deliver continuous improvement, and sustainable impact
  • Your experience of functional and corporate leadership reflecting the needs of diverse communities

You will also be asked to provide a personal statement explaining why you want to become a director.

How to apply for the ADP

Programme description

The programme consists of a blend of virtual and face-to-face learning. There will also be the kind of informal interactions that you would get from a residential programme.

You can expect:

  • 8 taught days
  • Learning visits
  • Action learning
  • Leadership challenge activities
  • Access to leaders in the field

You will be assigned a mentor and an employer sponsor. You will also be offered coaching, career advice and support once the course has finished.

Evaluation and assessment

You’ll have formative assessments, including 360 feedback. You can also choose to have your work academically accredited.
