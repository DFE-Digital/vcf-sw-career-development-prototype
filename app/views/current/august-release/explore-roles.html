Important

Practitioner

Newly qualified social worker

Newly qualified social workers are social workers in their first 2 years of practice. They may get extra support through the assessed and supported year in employment (ASYE) programme.

  • Salary range: £24,000 to £28,000

Social worker

Child and family social workers assess risk, protect children, provide support to parents and establish relationships with all family members.

  • Salary range: £28,000 to £40,000

Experienced practitioner

Senior practitioner

Senior practitioners manage complex caseloads, provide quality assurance and mentor and support less experienced social workers.

  • Salary range: £35,000 to £50,000

Practice supervisor

Practice supervisors support the practice and decision-making of social workers and develop their skills as individuals and teams.

  • Salary range: £35,000 to £55,000

Manager

Practice development manager

Practice development managers lead the development of social work teams to promote and support evidence-based best practice

  • Salary range: £40,000 to £55,000

Quality improvement manager

Quality improvement managers audit and analyse the performance of social workers, teams and processes to ensure the delivery of high-quality social care.

  • Salary range: £38,000 to £65,000

Child protection advisor

Child protection advisors provide specialist guidance, support and leadership to ensure the safety and wellbeing of children.

  • Salary range: £38,000 to £45,000

Team manager

Team managers lead, develop and support a group of social workers to assess and manage risk to children and families.

  • Salary range: £45,000 to £65,000

Independent reviewing officer

Independent reviewing officers oversee and review care plans to ensure local authorities are acting in the best interests of children.

  • Salary range: £42,000 to £57,000

Senior manager

Principal social worker

Principal social workers support social work teams, provide quality assurance and contribute to strategic leadership.

  • Salary range: £50,000 to £65,000

Service manager

Service managers oversee the performance and quality of practice of multiple teams of social workers across a service area.

  • Salary range: £46,000 to £70,000

Head of service

Heads of service oversee the day-to-day operations of services, manage staff and work closely with other agencies to promote best practice in social work.

  • Salary range: £60,000 to £80,000

Leader

Assistant director

Assistant directors oversee the strategic development, management, and delivery of services that support and protect children and families.

  • Salary range: £70,000 to £90,000

Director of children's services

Directors of children’s services lead, manage and take overall responsibility for the delivery of high-quality social care in their local authority.

  • Salary range: £100,000 to £150,000
