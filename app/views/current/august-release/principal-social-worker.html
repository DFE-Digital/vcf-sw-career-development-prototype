Principal social worker

Salary range
£45,000 to £65,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Manage complex cases, support social work teams and contribute to the strategic leadership of services
Other names
Lead social worker, advanced practitioner, practice development lead, consultant social worker

What you'll do

As a principal social worker you’ll:

  • provide leadership and guidance to social work teams, promoting best practice and high-quality service delivery
  • support social workers to ensure their professional development and well-being
  • offer expert consultation and guidance on complex cases and help social workers make informed decisions
  • review and monitor cases to ensure professional standards and policies are met
  • contribute to the development of policies, procedures, and practice frameworks within your organisation
  • organise training and professional development opportunities for social workers
  • develop and maintain effective working relationships with multi-agency partners
  • stay up to date with research, legislation and best practices

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership skills to lead and motivate social work teams
  • in-depth knowledge of child development, family dynamics and safeguarding
  • critical thinking and decision-making skills to analyse complex situations, assess risks and make informed decisions
  • strategic thinking to develop long-term plans and achieve organisational goals
  • communication skills to share information, provide feedback and work with diverse stakeholders
  • the ability to prioritise tasks and delegate responsibilities to ensure deadlines are met
  • an understanding of policy frameworks and practice standards

How to become one

To become a principal social worker you must:

  • have a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • have at least 5 years’ experience as a qualified child and family social worker
  • have leadership and management experience in a social work setting

You’ll also need to have passed enhanced background checks.

If you’re an experienced service manager or head of service, the Pathway 4: practice leaders programme can help prepare you for promotion to a principal social worker role.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could move into a higher-level leadership position such as assistant director or director of children’s services.

The upon: aspirant directors programme can help prepare you for promotion to a director of children’s services role.
