Team manager

Salary range
£45,000 to £65,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Lead, develop and support a team of social workers to assess and manage risk to children and families
Other names
Middle manager, practice manager, team leader

What you'll do

As a team manager you’ll:

  • manage a team of social workers and support their professional and personal development
  • assign cases and provide oversight and quality assurance by leading regular supervision sessions
  • conduct performance reviews and give feedback to team members
  • chair and attend statutory meetings
  • represent your team at meetings with senior management and other stakeholders
  • manage your team's budgets and resources
  • identify training needs and provide learning opportunities to support team members' professional development
  • handle complaints and concerns from service users and their families
  • uphold and strengthen professional standards in your team

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership and management skills to lead and motivate a team of social workers
  • decision-making and problem-solving skills to handle complex and sensitive cases
  • excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • a strong understanding of child development, safeguarding and child protection issues
  • knowledge of different intervention strategies and evidence-based practices
  • the ability to support social workers professionally and emotionally
  • knowledge of self-care practices and managing personal and team stress

How to become one

To become a team manager you must:

  • have a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • have at least 5 years' experience as a registered child and family social worker
  • have experience supervising social workers and advising on their practice

Some employers may prefer candidates who have a post-graduate qualification in practice education.

If you’re an experienced practitioner, the Pathway 2: middle managers programme can help prepare you for promotion to a team manager role.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could:

  • progress into an advanced leadership role such as principal social worker, service manager or head of service
  • move into a training and consultancy role and deliver training programmes to social workers
  • specialise in quality assurance and compliance, ensuring standards and regulatory requirements are met

The Pathway 3: heads of service programme can help prepare you for promotion to a head of service role.
