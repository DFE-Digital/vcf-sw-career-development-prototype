Practice supervisor

Salary range
£35,000 to £50,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Supervise the practice and decision-making of child and family practitioners and develop the skills of individuals and teams
Other names
Practice development manager, practice development consultant, practice development officer

What you'll do

As a practice supervisor you'll:

  • support social workers to ensure best practice and compliance with policies and procedures
  • provide a space for social workers to reflect on and discuss their work with children and families
  • provide quality assurance of work done by social workers such as assessments, court statements and care plans
  • offer guidance on complex case assessments, risk management and safeguarding
  • give feedback to social workers about their strengths and areas where they can improve
  • review and monitor caseloads to ensure timely and effective service delivery
  • provide training and professional development opportunities to enhance the skills and knowledge of social workers
  • work with multi-agency partners within the child welfare system
  • work with team managers to develop policies and strategies to improve social work practice

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership skills to guide and inspire social work practitioners in delivering effective child and family support
  • knowledge of risk assessment and safeguarding procedures
  • knowledge of legislation and policies relating to the welfare of children
  • the ability to build effective relationships with colleagues, families and multi-agency partners
  • the ability to give constructive feedback to staff
  • knowledge of child development theories and a range of social work interventions
  • the ability to work with diverse service users and understand the impact of cultural factors on families
  • analytical skills to evaluate complex cases and identify the needs of children and families

How to become one

To become a practice supervisor you must:

  • have a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • have at least 4 years' post-qualifying experience as a registered child and family social worker
  • have experience of mentoring and supporting others to improve their practice

It’s helpful to have experience as a practice educator, providing guidance and support to social work students during their placements.

You’ll also need to have passed enhanced background checks.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could become:

  • a senior practitioner and specialise in an area such as child protection or adoption
  • a team manager and oversee a social work team
  • a service manager and oversee the operations of a social work service area

You can develop your practice and leadership skills by doing the Pathway 1: practice supervisors programme. It’s funded by the Department for Education and provided by Frontline.
