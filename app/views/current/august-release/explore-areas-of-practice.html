Explore areas of practice

Areas of practice are teams within child and family social work. Your responsibilities and day to day may differ depending on your area of practice. Find out more about what specific areas of practices entail.

Child Protection & Family Safeguarding

Family Safeguarding is a strengths-based approach to working with families, aiming to help them stay together and ensure the safety of children. It involves multi-disciplinary joint teams, including children's social work, community mental health staff, domestic abuse specialists, and substance misuse workers.

Fostering and adoption

Fostering and Adoption teams assess, support, and monitor prospective foster and adoptive parents throughout the application and approval process.

Youth offending

Youth Offending Team (YOT) is a specialised unit within social work that focuses on working with children and young people who are involved in the criminal justice system or at risk of offending.
