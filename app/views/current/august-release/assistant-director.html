Assistant director

Salary range
£88,000 to £98,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Oversee the strategic development, management and delivery of services that support and protect children and families
Other names
Deputy director

What you'll do

As an assistant director you’ll:

  • provide strategic leadership and direction to drive the development of child and family social work services
  • work with senior management to plan and deliver services that meet the needs of children and families
  • help develop and install policies and procedures to ensure consistent and effective service delivery
  • oversee the performance of social work teams and ensure professional standards and regulatory requirements are met
  • ensure strong safeguarding practices and risk assessment processes are in place to protect the safety of service users
  • build and maintain partnerships with multi-agency partners to ensure co-ordinated service delivery
  • plan, manage and monitor the allocation of budget and resources
  • evaluate practices, systems and procedures and advise on how to improve them

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership and management skills to provide strategic direction and manage resources effectively
  • strategic thinking to drive service improvement and achieve organisational goals
  • the ability to make sound judgements on key issues in a political environment
  • in-depth knowledge of social work practice including legislation, policies and trends in the field
  • communication and interpersonal skills to engage with diverse stakeholders and build partnerships
  • knowledge of budgeting, resource allocation and financial management
  • the ability to monitor, evaluate and manage performance to ensure quality service provision
  • data analysis and reporting skills to analyse complex data, interpret findings and prepare reports

How to become one

To become an assistant director you must:

  • have a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • have extensive experience in child and family social work practice
  • have experience of working at management level in a multi-agency environment

You’ll also need to have passed enhanced background checks.

If you’re an experienced head of service or equivalent, the Pathway 4: practice leaders programme can help prepare you for promotion to an assistant director role.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could progress to the role of director of children’s services.

The upon: aspirant directors programme can help prepare you for promotion to a director of children’s services role.

You could also move into a senior leadership position in a social work organisation such as chief executive officer.
