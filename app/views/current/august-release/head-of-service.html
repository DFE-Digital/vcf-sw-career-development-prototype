Head of service

Salary range
£60,000 to £90,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Oversee the day-to-day operations of services, manage staff and work with other agencies to promote best practice in social work
Other names
Service manager, practice lead

What you'll do

As a head of service you’ll:

  • oversee and lead social work teams to ensure effective service delivery
  • set the strategic direction of the service and identify areas for improvement
  • oversee and develop partnerships with other statutory services including health, police and education
  • carry out reporting to external stakeholders
  • allocate and manage resources including budgets and staff
  • support the professional development of social workers by promoting training and continuous learning
  • set performance objectives, conduct reviews and address issues within social work teams
  • stay up to date with research, best practices and emerging trends in the field

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership skills to guide and inspire social work teams, set strategic direction and drive positive change
  • strong written and verbal communication skills to engage with teams and stakeholders
  • knowledge of policies and legislation governing child and family social work services
  • an understanding of case management principles, risk assessment and safeguarding practices
  • analytical and critical thinking skills to assess risk and resolve complex issues
  • resilience and emotional intelligence to manage high-pressure situations and support staff well-being

How to become one

To become a head of service you must:

  • have a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • have significant post-qualifying experience as a qualified child and family social worker
  • have leadership and management experience in a social work setting

You’ll also need to have passed enhanced background checks.

If you’re an experienced team manager, the Pathway 3: heads of service programme can help prepare you for promotion to a head of service role.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could move into a higher-level leadership position such as assistant director or director of children’s services.

The upon: aspirant directors programme can help prepare you for promotion to a director of children’s services role.
