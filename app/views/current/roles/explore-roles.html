Explore roles

Find out about different roles at each career stage, what they involve and the skills and qualifications you’ll need.

Practitioner

Newly qualified social worker (opens a new website)

Newly qualified social workers are social workers in their first 2 years of practice. They may get extra support through the assessed and supported year in employment (ASYE) programme.

  • Salary range: £24,000 to £28,000
Social worker (opens a new website)

Child and family social workers assess risk, protect children, provide support to parents and establish relationships with all family members.

  • Salary range: £28,000 to £40,000

Experienced practitioner

Senior practitioner (opens a new website)

Senior practitioners manage complex caseloads, provide quality assurance and mentor and support less experienced social workers.

  • Salary range: £38,000 to £50,000
Supervisor (opens a new website)

Supervisors oversee the practice and decision-making of child and family practitioners and work with them to develop their skills.

  • Salary range: £35,000 to £55,000

Manager

Quality improvement manager (opens a new website)

Quality improvement managers audit and analyse the performance of social workers, teams and processes to ensure the delivery of high-quality social care.

  • Salary range: £38,000 to £65,000
Child protection advisor (opens a new website)

Child protection advisors provide specialist guidance, support and leadership to ensure the safety and wellbeing of children.

  • Salary range: £38,000 to £45,000
Team manager (opens a new website)

Team managers lead, develop and support a group of social workers to assess and manage risk to children and families.

  • Salary range: £45,000 to £65,000

Senior manager

Principal social worker (opens a new website)

Principal social workers manage complex cases, support social work teams and contribute to the strategic leadership of services.

  • Salary range: £50,000 to £65,000
Service manager (opens a new website)

Service managers oversee the performance and quality of practice of multiple teams of social workers across a service area.

  • Salary range: £46,000 to £70,000
Head of service (opens a new website)

Heads of service are responsible for the strategic leadership and delivery of services through management of social work teams and relationships with multi-agency partners.

  • Salary range: £60,000 to £80,000

Leader

Assistant director (opens a new website)

Assistant directors set the strategic direction of children’s services and lead and manage operational teams.

  • Salary range: £70,000 to £90,000
Director of children's services (opens a new website)

Directors of children’s service lead, manage and take overall responsibility for the delivery of high-quality social care in their local authority.

  • Salary range: £100,000 to £150,000
