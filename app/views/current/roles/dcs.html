Director of children's services

Salary range
£100,000 to £150,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Lead, manage and take overall responsibility for the delivery of high-quality social care in a local authority
Other names
Director of children’s social care

What you'll do

As a director of children’s services you’ll:

  • provide strategic leadership of children's services to align with national policies and local authority objectives
  • oversee the planning, development and delivery of services that meet the needs of children and families
  • manage the budget and resources allocated to children's services, ensuring they're used efficiently and effectively
  • build and maintain effective partnerships with stakeholders including schools, health services and community organisations
  • set targets and implement measures to achieve continuous improvement in service quality
  • help develop and implement policies and initiatives that promote the welfare of children and young people
  • manage a team of professionals, providing guidance and development opportunities to ensure high standards of practice
  • work with multi-agency partners to promote collaborative working and information sharing to improve outcomes for service users

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership skills to provide strategic direction, motivate teams and manage resources and budgets
  • strategic thinking to analyse complex issues and make informed decisions for service improvement
  • in-depth knowledge of legislation, regulations and policies related to children's services and social care
  • budgeting, resource allocation and financial management skills to ensure effective use of resources
  • interpersonal and communication skills to build relationships, collaborate and negotiate with diverse stakeholders
  • knowledge of safeguarding principles and child protection protocols to ensure the well-being of children and young people
  • the ability to evaluate service performance and put in place quality assurance measures that drive continuous improvement

How to become one

To become a director of children’s services you'll ideally have:

  • a degree in social work or a related field
  • a professional qualification such as the Level 7 Diploma in Management and Leadership
  • at least 10 years’ relevant experience in a senior management role
  • experience leading multidisciplinary teams, implementing strategic plans and driving organisational change
  • experience in budget management and resource allocation

You’ll also need to have passed enhanced background checks.

If you’re an assistant director, the upon: aspirant directors programme can help prepare you for promotion to a director of children's services role.

Career paths and progression

The upon: new directors programme can help new directors of children’s services develop their leadership, management and technical skills.

With experience, you could move into a senior leadership position in a social work organisation such as chief executive officer or chief operating officer.
