Assesment and intake

Assessment and intake teams carry out initial assessments with children and families to identify risk and determine appropriate support.

Fostering and adoption

Fostering and adoption teams facilitate the placement of children into safe and stable family environments and provide ongoing support.

Youth offending

Youth offending teams work with young people at risk of offending to provide interventions and minimise the risk of re-offending.

Looked after children

Looked after children teams support and care for children who are looked after by the local authorities wether they are in foster care, residential care, or placed with relatives under a care order.

Children with disabilities

Children with disabilities team provide support and assistance to individuals and families with disabilities, with a focus on promoting independence, wellbeing, and positive outcomes.

Mental health

Mental health teams support the mental well-being of children and young people, promoting positive family relationships, and ensuring a safe and nurturing environment for their growth and development

Adolescents

Adolescents teams are generally responsible for providing social work support to young people and their families who are facing various challenges or issues during adolescence.

Asylum and refugees

Asylum and refugee teams provide specialised support and assistance to asylum-seeking and refugee families, particularly those with children.

International social work

The international social work team's primary focus is on families with complex international connections, including families with members residing in different countries and those with a history of migration or cross-border issues.
