Principal social worker

Salary range
£45,000 to £70,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Oversee the performance and quality of practice of multiple social work teams across a service area
Other names
Service lead, area manager

What you'll do

As a service manager you’ll:

  • oversee and lead social work teams to ensure effective service delivery
  • manage team resources including caseloads, staffing and budgets
  • monitor and evaluate service outcomes and identify areas for improvement
  • report on service performance and provide strategic input to senior stakeholders
  • work closely with multi-agency partners and other local authorities
  • ensure services are delivered in line with relevant policies and legal requirements
  • undertake performance reviews and audits to ensure services are delivered to a high standard

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • strong leadership and management skills to motivate and inspire teams of social workers
  • knowledge of child protection legislation, policies and best practice guidelines
  • a strategic mindset to drive service improvement and innovation
  • knowledge of social work theories, methods and evidence-based interventions
  • financial management skills to oversee budgets and resource allocation
  • experience in managing performance and supporting professional development
  • strong organisational skills to manage multiple priorities and deadlines

How to become one

To become a service manager you must have:

  • a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • significant post-qualifying experience as a qualified social worker

You’ll also need to pass enhanced background checks.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could move into a higher-level leadership position such as:

  • head of service
  • assistant director
  • director of children's services
