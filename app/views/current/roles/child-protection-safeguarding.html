Areas of practice

Child protection and safeguarding

Summary
Work with families and external partners to promote the welfare of children and protect them from harm
Other names
Family safeguarding, assessment and intervention, family support and safeguarding

What you'll do

Your responsibilities will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in child protection and safeguarding teams are expected to:

  • carry out child protection assessments where there are concerns about child abuse, neglect or mental health issues
  • gather information from interviews and observations to determine risk and develop appropriate interventions
  • collaborate with families to develop safety plans to protect children from harm
  • provide crisis intervention when immediate action is needed to keep children safe
  • work with partner agencies to coordinate, share information and carry out multi-agency assessments
  • coordinate child protection conferences to discuss concerns with families and plan actions
  • carry out home visits, observe family dynamics and assess child well-being
  • support families to make positive changes and connect them with relevant services
  • advocate for the best interests of children in court by compiling reports and giving evidence

Skills and knowledge

The skills and knowledge you need will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in child protection and safeguarding teams are expected to have:

  • knowledge of the requirements of safeguarding children and young people
  • awareness of relationship-based social work, assessments and interventions
  • knowledge of the principles of participatory practice with children and young people
  • an understanding of relevant legislation, policies and frameworks relating to child safeguarding
  • knowledge of mental health, substance abuse, child sexual exploitation and domestic violence issues in social work
  • experience of care proceedings and private law

It could also be useful to have:

  • an advanced qualification in social work such as a higher specialist award in working with children and families
  • group supervision skills, such as experience of facilitating group supervision

Who you'll work with

You might work with external partners and services including:

  • police and emergency services
  • health services
  • schools
  • housing departments
  • substance misuse workers
  • domestic abuse specialists

How you'll work

Working conditions can vary, but you could:

  • be office-based on work remotely, or a combination of both
  • work full-time or part-time
  • work flexible hours, compressed hours or have a job share
