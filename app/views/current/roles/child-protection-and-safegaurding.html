Areas of practice

Child Protection & Family Safeguarding

Summary
Work with families and external partners to promote the welfare of children and protect them from harm
Other names
Family safeguarding, assessment and intervention, family support and safeguarding

What you'll do

Your responsibilities will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in child protection and safeguarding teams are expected to:

  • carry out child protection assessments where there are concerns about child abuse, neglect or mental health issues
  • gather information from interviews and observations to determine risk and develop appropriate interventions
  • collaborate with families to develop safety plans to protect children from harm
  • provide crisis intervention when immediate action is needed to keep children safe
  • work with partner agencies to coordinate, share information and carry out multi-agency assessments
  • coordinate child protection conferences to discuss concerns with families and plan actions
  • carry out home visits, observe family dynamics and assess child well-being
  • support families to make positive changes and connect them with relevant services
  • advocate for the best interests of children in court by compiling reports and giving evidence
