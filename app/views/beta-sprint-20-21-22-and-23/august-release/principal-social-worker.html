Principal social worker

Salary range
£45,000 to £65,000 depending on location, responsibilities and experience
Summary
Support social work teams, provide quality assurance and contribute to strategic leadership
Other names
Lead social worker, advanced practitioner, practice development lead, consultant social worker

What you'll do

As a principal social worker you’ll:

  • provide leadership to social workers and represent their views to senior stakeholders
  • support social workers with their professional development and well-being
  • provide guidance and quality assurance to help navigate complex decision-making
  • monitor cases to ensure professional standards and policies are met
  • work with team managers to allocate resources, manage workloads and monitor performance
  • develop evidence-based training that's fixed to relevant practice models
  • contribute to the development of policies, procedures, and practice models within your organisation
  • develop and maintain effective working relationships with multi-agency partners

Skills and knowledge

You'll need:

  • leadership skills to lead and motivate social workers
  • in-depth knowledge of child development, family dynamics and safeguarding
  • critical thinking skills to analyse complex situations and make informed decisions
  • strategic thinking to develop long-term plans and achieve organisational goals
  • communication skills to navigate dynamics between social workers and senior stakeholders
  • the ability to prioritise tasks and delegate responsibilities to ensure deadlines are met
  • an awareness of national policy and how it affects social work teams

How to become one

To become a principal social worker you must:

  • have a degree in social work
  • be registered with Social Work England
  • have at least 5 years’ experience as a qualified child and family social worker
  • have leadership and management experience in a social work setting
  • have experience collaborating with external partners and influencing practice outside your organisation

You’ll also need to have passed enhanced background checks.

If you’re an experienced service manager or head of service, the Pathway 4: practice leaders programme can help prepare you for promotion to a principal social worker role.

Career paths and progression

With experience you could:

  • progress into an advanced leadership role such as head of service, assistant director or director of children's services
  • move into a training and consultancy role and deliver training programmes to social workers
  • become an expert in your specialist area of social work practice

The upon: aspirant directors programme can help prepare you for promotion to a director of children’s services role.
