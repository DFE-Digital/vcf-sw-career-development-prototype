Overview

The assessed and supported year in employment (ASYE) programme helps newly qualified social workers develop their skills, knowledge and professional confidence.

ASYE is open to all newly qualified social workers employed in the public, private and voluntary sectors.

How you're assessed

A qualified social worker will support, assess and record your progress throughout the programme. They’ll do this on their own or with others such as your line manager or ASYE coordinator.

You’ll be assessed against the post-qualifying standards for child and family practitioners.

Evidence of your professional development will be collected through:

  • direct observations
  • feedback from children and young people who need care and support
  • feedback from other professionals
  • assessment of written reports

You’ll also need to complete critical reflection logs after 3, 6 and 12 months. Your employer can also request an optional 9-month review of your progress.

At the end of the year your assessor will write a final assessment which your employer will use to decide whether you’ve met the relevant standards.

How you're supported

During your ASYE you'll have:

  • regular supervision
  • a reduced caseload
  • protected time for critical reflection

Depending on your employer:

  • you'll have half a day per week of protected development time
  • your workload will be 10% less than an experienced social worker’s

You may also have the opportunity to shadow experienced social workers and go on placements.
