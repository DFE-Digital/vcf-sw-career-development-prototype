What you'll do

Your responsibilities will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in fostering and adoption teams are expected to:

  • recruit prospective foster or adoptive families and assess their suitability through home visits, interviews and background checks
  • provide support to prospective families to help them understand the process and expectations
  • facilitate the placement process to ensure the best interests of children are met
  • collaborate with other professionals to identify suitable matches between children and prospective families
  • regularly visit children to check on their well-being and address any concerns or issues
  • provide ongoing support to families to ensure permanency and stability for children
  • evaluate the effectiveness of families and make recommendations for changes
  • collaborate with external partners to ensure coordinated support for children and families
  • advocate for the best interests of children in court by compiling reports and giving evidence

Skills and knowledge

The skills and knowledge you need will vary depending on your role and position in the team.

Social workers in fostering and adoption teams are expected to have:

  • the ability to conduct assessments, facilitate training and provide support to families
  • an understanding of relevant legislation and policies relating to fostering, adoption and guardianship
  • analytical and report writing skills to facilitate decision-making
  • an understanding of relevant legislation, policies and frameworks relating to child safeguarding
  • an understanding of child development and the impact of adverse childhood experiences
  • an understanding of parenting capacity, environmental, risk and protective factors

Who you'll work with

You could work with external partners and services including:

  • fostering and adoption agencies
  • residential care homes
  • health services
  • schools
  • housing departments
  • legal professionals

How you'll work
