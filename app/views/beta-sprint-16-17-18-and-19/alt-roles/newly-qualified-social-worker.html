Newly qualified social worker

Salary range
£38,000 to £50,000
Responsibilities
Manage complex caseloads, provide quality assurance and support less experienced social workers
Other names
Advanced practitioner, practice consultant, practice development lead, practice educator, senior social worker

What you'll do

Discover the day-to-day tasks you'll do in this role.

Day-to-day tasks

You'll need to:

  • conduct assessments of children and families to identify needs and risks
  • develop care plans that address the needs of children and families and promote their well-being
  • provide emotional and practical support to children and families
  • work with other social workers and healthcare and education professionals
  • keep up-to-date records of assessment, care plans and progress reports
  • follow the relevant legal and professional standards
  • have regular supervision sessions with managers
  • take part in training and development to enhance your skills and knowledge

What it takes

Find out what skills you'll use in this role.

Skills and knowledge

You'll need to:

  • communicate clearly and effectively
  • understand and empathise with the challenges faced by children and families who are experiencing difficulties
  • analyse complex situations when conducting assessments
  • manage multiple cases at the same time
  • deal with difficult cases and situations
  • work effectively with diverse service users and colleagues
  • prioritise and respond to emerging issues in cases
